# Traveler Rules

## Purpose
This document defines how to adapt packing recommendations based on traveler demographics, special needs, and multi-traveler coordination.

## Core Principle
**Pack for the specific travelers on the trip, not a generic template.**

## Gender-Specific Packing Needs

### Male Travelers

**Standard Considerations:**
- Typically fewer clothing items
- Heavier/bulkier footwear on average
- Simplified toiletries
- Less variety in outfit combinations

**Clothing Quantities (7-day trip baseline):**
- Underwear: 7-8 pairs
- Socks: 7-8 pairs
- T-shirts/Casual shirts: 5-6
- Pants/Shorts: 3-4
- Sleepwear: 1-2
- Outerwear: Based on climate

**Weight Characteristics:**
- Men's clothing: Generally 15-20% heavier than equivalent women's
- Men's shoes: Typically 800-1200g per pair vs. 400-800g women's

**Special Items:**
- Shaving kit (electric or manual)
- Cologne (optional, small bottle)
- Grooming tools (trimmer, razor)

### Female Travelers

**Standard Considerations:**
- More clothing variety expected socially
- Wider range of accessories
- More comprehensive toiletries
- Outfit versatility important
- Layering pieces

**Clothing Quantities (7-day trip baseline):**
- Underwear: 7-8 pairs
- Socks: 7-8 pairs
- Tops: 6-8 (variety of styles)
- Bottoms: 4-5 (pants, skirts, shorts, dresses)
- Sleepwear: 1-2
- Outerwear: Based on climate
- Dresses: 2-3 (if appropriate for trip)

**Special Items:**
- Cosmetics (if worn)
- Hair care products and tools
- Accessories (jewelry, scarves, bags)
- Feminine hygiene products (full trip supply + extra)
- Additional undergarments (bras, shapewear)

**Weight Considerations:**
- Women's clothing: Generally lighter but more items
- Accessories add: 500-1000g typically
- Toiletries/cosmetics: +500g to 1kg vs. men's

### Non-Binary / Prefer Not to Say

**Approach:**
- Ask about specific preferences if not volunteered
- Provide flexible, versatile packing suggestions
- Include items from all categories as appropriate
- Focus on climate and activity needs
- Don't make assumptions about style or products needed

## Age-Based Requirements

### Infants (0-12 months)

**Unique Items:**
- Diapers (estimate 8-10 per day or plan to buy)
  - Pack for 2 days + buy locally to save weight
  - Weight: ~200g per diaper
- Baby wipes (essential)
- Formula/bottles (if not breastfeeding)
- Baby food (if eating solids)
- Changing pad (portable)
- Diaper cream
- Baby carrier/sling

**Clothing (quantities per day Ã— trip days):**
- Onesies: 2-3 per day (blowout backup)
- Sleep sacks: 1-2
- Warm layer: 1-2
- Socks: Multiple pairs (easily lost)
- Hats: 2 (sun and warm)
- Bibs: 3-4

**Special Considerations:**
- Temperature regulation critical (babies can't self-regulate)
- Pack extra of everything (messes happen)
- Formula/food: Consider bringing full supply (availability concerns)
- Accommodation: Confirm crib/high chair availability
- Laundry: Essential for extended trips

**Weight Impact:**
- Infant supplies: 5-8 kg for week trip
- Recommend: Parents balance their personal items

### Toddlers (1-3 years)

**Unique Items:**
- Diapers or pull-ups (if not potty trained)
- Portable potty seat (if recently trained)
- Snacks (travel-friendly, familiar)
- Comfort items (stuffed animal, blanket)
- Entertainment (small toys, books)
- Stroller (often gate-checked)

**Clothing:**
- Multiple outfits per day (spills, messes)
- Swimming diapers (if beach/pool)
- Comfortable shoes (lots of walking)
- Jacket/sweater (temperature changes)

**Special Considerations:**
- Backup clothing in carry-on essential
- Familiar foods (picky eating)
- Nap schedule items
- Safety items (outlet covers, door locks for accommodations)

**Weight Impact:**
- Toddler supplies: 6-10 kg for week trip

### Children (4-12 years)

**Unique Items:**
- Entertainment (devices, books, toys)
- Medications (children's formulations)
- Sunscreen (child-safe formulation)
- Snacks (longer than toddlers without, but still helpful)
- Sports/activity gear (if planned)

**Clothing:**
- Can wear items 2-3 times like adults
- Growing: Don't overpack (may grow during trip)
- Activity-appropriate (playground, hiking, swimming)
- Comfortable walking shoes (critical)
- Rain gear (kids less tolerant of being wet)

**Special Considerations:**
- Age 6+: Can manage small backpack as personal item
- Swimming: Always pack swim gear (kids love pools)
- First aid: Kid-specific (band-aids with characters, children's pain reliever)
- Safety: ID tags, emergency contact info
- Comfort: Favorite items for unfamiliar places

**Weight Impact:**
- Child supplies: 4-8 kg (age-dependent)
- Ages 8+: Can carry small backpack (2-3 kg)

### Teenagers (13-17 years)

**Unique Items:**
- Electronics (phone, earbuds, chargers, probably more than adults)
- Skin care (often more extensive in teen years)
- School-age: May need study materials
- Style-conscious: More outfit variety

**Clothing:**
- Similar to adults
- Fashion preferences important (morale)
- Athleisure popular
- Multiple shoes (style variety)

**Special Considerations:**
- Can manage own packing with guidance
- Own carry-on and personal item
- Technology: Emphasize chargers, adapters
- Autonomy: Respect their input on packing
- Social: Appropriate clothing for photos/social media

**Weight Impact:**
- Teen supplies: 8-12 kg (similar to adult)
- Can carry their own luggage

### Adults (18-64 years)

**Standard Baseline:**
- Full independence in packing
- Typical weight: 8-15 kg total
- Standard clothing formulas apply
- Toiletries: Personal preference

**Activity Level Considerations:**
- **Sedentary Trip:** Comfortable walking shoes sufficient
- **Moderate Activity:** One athletic shoe, activity gear
- **Active/Adventure:** Specialized gear, multiple footwear, 15-20 kg

**Work/Business:**
- Professional clothing (heavier, less versatile)
- Laptop and accessories
- Business documents
- Garment bag (if suits)
- Weight: +2-4 kg for business materials

### Seniors (65+ years)

**Special Considerations:**
- Mobility aids (cane, walker - usually free on airline)
- Medications (full supply + extra + documentation)
- Comfort prioritized (proper shoes, cushions)
- Medical documentation
- Compression socks (for flight)
- Easier access packing (rolling vs. backpack)

**Packing Adaptations:**
- Lighter luggage (easier to manage)
- Wheeled bags essential
- Less bending (packing cubes at top)
- Medications in carry-on + checked backup
- Emergency contacts and medical info (laminated card)

**Special Medical Items:**
- CPAP machine (doesn't count toward baggage)
- Hearing aids (bring extra batteries)
- Glasses (bring backup pair)
- Medications (bring double + prescription copies)
- Blood pressure cuff, glucose monitor, etc. as needed

**Weight Impact:**
- Similar to adults, but carried differently
- Prioritize wheeled luggage
- Consider: Luggage shipping to avoid carrying

## Number of Travelers Coordination

### Solo Traveler

**Advantages:**
- All luggage allowance for one person
- Flexible packing
- Can carry own bags

**Packing Strategy:**
- Maximize efficiency
- Can go carry-on only easier
- All space is yours

### Couple (2 People)

**Advantages:**
- Share items: Toiletries, first aid, adapters
- Distribute weight across two allowances
- Can specialize bags (clothes in one, gear in other)

**Packing Strategy:**
- **Shared Items in One Bag:**
  - Toiletries (share shampoo, toothpaste, etc.)
  - First aid kit
  - Travel adapters
  - Laundry supplies
- **Individual Items Split:**
  - Each person's clothing
  - Personal medications
  - Personal electronics

**Weight Distribution:**
- Example: Two 23kg allowances = 46kg total
- Distribute heavy shared items evenly
- Each carries personal essentials in carry-on (redundancy)

**Efficiency Gains:**
- Save: 1-2kg by sharing toiletries
- Save: Space by sharing adapters, first aid
- Can pack more specialty items

### Family (3-4 People)

**Coordination Critical:**
- Strategic packing across multiple bags
- Shared family items
- Mix-and-match if one bag lost

**Packing Strategy:**
- **Family Shared Items:**
  - Toiletries (one set for family)
  - First aid (comprehensive kit)
  - Electronics/adapters (one set)
  - Beach/activity gear
  - Laundry supplies
- **Individual Items:**
  - Each person's clothing
  - Personal medications
  - Comfort items (for kids)

**Weight Distribution Example (Family of 4):**
```
Parent 1: 22kg checked + carry-on
  - Parent 1 clothing: 6kg
  - Child 1 clothing: 4kg
  - Shared toiletries: 2kg
  - Shared gear: 10kg

Parent 2: 22kg checked + carry-on
  - Parent 2 clothing: 6kg
  - Child 2 clothing: 4kg
  - More shared gear: 12kg

Child 1: Carry-on only (3kg)
  - Personal items, entertainment, comfort items

Child 2: Carry-on only (3kg)
  - Personal items, entertainment, comfort items

Total: 44kg checked + 6kg carry-on = 50kg
```

**Risk Mitigation:**
- Each bag has at least one person's complete outfit (in case of loss)
- Critical items split across bags (don't put all medications in one bag)
- Kids carry change of clothes in carry-on

### Large Group (5+ People)

**Significant Coordination:**
- Designate "group gear" carriers
- Share bulky items extensively
- Spreadsheet recommended for tracking

**Shared Items Across Group:**
- One comprehensive first aid kit
- Limited adapter sets (share in rooms)
- Group activity gear
- Beach/swimming supplies

**Weight Advantages:**
- Large total allowance
- Can bring group items (portable speaker, games, sports gear)
- More flexibility for purchases

## Special Needs Accommodations

### Medical Conditions

**Diabetes:**
- Insulin (temperature-controlled carry bag)
- Glucose monitor and strips
- Snacks (manage blood sugar)
- Medical documentation/prescription
- Extra supplies (double what needed)

**Allergies (Severe/Food):**
- EpiPen (carry-on, never checked)
- Antihistamines
- Safe snacks (if food allergies)
- Allergy card in local language
- Medical alert bracelet

**Mobility Impairments:**
- Assistive devices (airline doesn't count toward baggage)
- Comfortable, supportive shoes
- Medications for pain management
- Documentation for equipment
- Easy-access packing

**Chronic Conditions:**
- Full medication supply + extra
- Prescription copies
- Doctor's note (for customs)
- Medical contact information
- Symptom management items

### Dietary Restrictions

**Impact on Packing:**
- Safe snacks (if availability uncertain)
- Supplements (if needed)
- Allergy cards (translated)
- Research destination food availability

**Examples:**
- Vegan: Protein bars, supplements
- Gluten-free: Safe snacks, allergy cards
- Kosher/Halal: May need to bring specific foods

### Accessibility Needs

**Vision Impairments:**
- Extra glasses/contacts
- Magnifiers or aids
- Prescription sunglasses
- Medications for eye conditions

**Hearing Impairments:**
- Extra hearing aid batteries
- Backup hearing aids if available
- Communication cards
- Phone for text communication

**Sensory Sensitivities:**
- Noise-canceling headphones
- Comfort items
- Familiar textures/clothing
- Sensory aids

## Multi-Traveler Luggage Optimization

### Shared Item Savings

**Example Calculations:**
```
SOLO TRAVELER:
  Toiletries: 1.5kg
  First Aid: 0.5kg
  Adapters: 0.3kg
  Total Shared: 2.3kg

COUPLE:
  Toiletries: 1.8kg (one set, slightly larger sizes)
  First Aid: 0.6kg
  Adapters: 0.3kg
  Total Shared: 2.7kg
  Per Person: 1.35kg
  SAVINGS PER PERSON: 0.95kg

FAMILY OF 4:
  Toiletries: 2.0kg (family sizes)
  First Aid: 0.8kg
  Adapters: 0.3kg
  Total Shared: 3.1kg
  Per Person: 0.775kg
  SAVINGS PER PERSON: 1.525kg
```

**Shared Item Strategy:**
- Larger sizes more efficient (one 200ml shampoo vs four 50ml)
- One comprehensive set vs. multiple partial sets
- Share electronics (one hair dryer, not four)

### Weight Distribution Tactics

**Balance Across Travelers:**
```
Example: Couple, one person has heavy business gear

Traveler A (business):
  - Laptop, documents: 3kg
  - Business clothing: 6kg
  - Personal items: 6kg
  - Total: 15kg

Traveler B:
  - Shared toiletries: 2kg
  - Both travelers' casual clothing: 10kg
  - Shared gear: 8kg
  - Total: 20kg

Result: Balanced load (15kg vs 20kg)
```

**Strategic Packing:**
- Heavier person can carry more comfortably
- Person with mobility issues carries less
- Distribute based on strength and comfort

## Family Packing Strategies

### Packing Cubes by Person
- Each family member gets a color
- Easy to find items
- Easy to distribute across bags
- Quick to reorganize

### Outfit Bundles for Kids
- Pre-match outfits (saves decision time)
- Roll together as units
- Kids can pick by bundle number
- Reduces morning stress

### Emergency Carry-On for Each Person
Every family member's carry-on includes:
- 1 complete outfit
- Essential medication
- Entertainment
- Snack
- Comfort item (for kids)

**Why:** If checked bags delayed, everyone can survive first day.

### Laundry Strategy for Families
- Essential for trips over 5 days
- Reduces clothing by 40-50%
- Kids' clothes: Small, quick to wash/dry
- Plan: Midpoint laundry or every 3-4 days

### Kid Entertainment Distribution
- Spread across carry-ons
- Share electronics (tablets with kid-friendly content)
- Include non-electronic (coloring books, small toys)
- Rotate to maintain interest

## Special Considerations by Traveler Profile

### First-Time International Travelers
- Extra guidance on customs, currencies, adapters
- Emphasize: Copies of documents
- Suggest: Small amount of local currency
- Recommend: Modest packing first time

### Experienced Travelers
- Less hand-holding
- Focus on trip-specific nuances
- Respect their packing expertise
- Optimize, don't over-explain basics

### Business Travelers
- Professional appearance critical
- Wrinkle-free packing techniques
- Laptop/technology emphasis
- Efficient packing (time-pressed)

### Adventure Travelers
- Specialized gear requirements
- Durability and functionality over style
- Weight distribution for active carrying
- Backup gear for critical items

### Luxury Travelers
- Quality over efficiency
- Comfort prioritized
- More outfits acceptable
- Don't stress weight minimization excessively

## Summary Guidelines

When creating packing recommendations:

1. **Identify each traveler** - age, gender, special needs
2. **Assess total group** - number, relationships, coordination
3. **Calculate shared item savings** - toiletries, gear
4. **Distribute weight strategically** - across travelers and bags
5. **Address special needs** - medical, dietary, accessibility
6. **Age-appropriate items** - infant supplies, kid entertainment, senior comfort
7. **Coordinate multi-bag packing** - risk mitigation, efficiency
8. **Communicate clearly** - who carries what, weight distribution
9. **Provide contingencies** - lost bag scenarios, emergency essentials
10. **Respect individual needs** - don't use rigid templates

**Remember:** Every traveler is unique. Use these guidelines as starting points, then adapt to specific situations and preferences.
