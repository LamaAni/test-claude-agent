# Climate Rules

## Purpose
Define how the Packing Advisor agent should interpret weather and climate data to make appropriate clothing and gear recommendations.

## Temperature to Clothing Type Mappings

### Extreme Cold: Below 0¬∞C (32¬∞F)
**Characteristics**: Freezing, potentially dangerous exposure

**Essential Items:**
- **Base layers**: Thermal underwear (top and bottom)
- **Mid layers**: Fleece or wool sweater
- **Outer layer**: Heavy insulated winter coat (down or synthetic)
- **Extremities**: 
  - Insulated gloves or mittens
  - Warm hat covering ears
  - Thermal socks (wool or synthetic)
  - Insulated boots
  - Scarf or neck gaiter
- **Accessories**: Hand/toe warmers for extended outdoor time

**Fabric priorities**: Wool, down, synthetic insulation, avoid cotton

**Activity modifications**: Limit time outdoors, layer heavily, cover all skin

### Cold: 0-10¬∞C (32-50¬∞F)
**Characteristics**: Chilly, requires layering

**Recommended Items:**
- **Base layer**: Long-sleeve shirt (wool or synthetic)
- **Mid layer**: Sweater or fleece
- **Outer layer**: Medium jacket (windproof, water-resistant)
- **Bottoms**: Long pants, jeans acceptable
- **Extremities**:
  - Light gloves
  - Hat or beanie
  - Closed-toe shoes, light boots
  - Regular or light wool socks

**Layering strategy**: 2-3 layers for outdoor activities

**Note**: Temperature feels colder with wind and rain

### Cool: 10-15¬∞C (50-59¬∞F)
**Characteristics**: Mild cold, comfortable with light layers

**Recommended Items:**
- **Top**: Long-sleeve shirts, light sweater
- **Outer layer**: Light jacket or cardigan
- **Bottoms**: Long pants, jeans
- **Footwear**: Closed-toe shoes, sneakers
- **Accessories**: Optional light scarf

**Layering strategy**: 1-2 layers, easy to add/remove

**Note**: Morning/evening may be cooler than midday

### Mild: 15-20¬∞C (59-68¬∞F)
**Characteristics**: Comfortable, pleasant weather

**Recommended Items:**
- **Tops**: Mix of short and long sleeves
- **Outer layer**: Light cardigan or jacket for evenings
- **Bottoms**: Pants or skirts
- **Footwear**: Any comfortable shoes
- **Accessories**: Minimal, optional light layers

**Versatility key**: Items that transition from day to evening

**Note**: Ideal temperature for most activities

### Warm: 20-25¬∞C (68-77¬∞F)
**Characteristics**: Warm, comfortable for shorts

**Recommended Items:**
- **Tops**: T-shirts, short sleeves, light blouses
- **Bottoms**: Shorts, skirts, light pants, dresses
- **Footwear**: Sandals, sneakers, breathable shoes
- **Optional**: Very light cardigan for air-conditioned spaces
- **Sun protection**: Sunglasses, hat

**Fabric priorities**: Cotton, linen, breathable synthetics

**Note**: Beginning of sun protection needs

### Hot: 25-32¬∞C (77-90¬∞F)
**Characteristics**: Hot, requires sun and heat management

**Recommended Items:**
- **Tops**: Lightweight, breathable shirts, tank tops
- **Bottoms**: Shorts, light skirts, breathable pants
- **Footwear**: Sandals, open-toed shoes, breathable sneakers
- **Sun protection**:
  - Wide-brimmed hat or cap
  - Sunglasses (UV protection)
  - Sunscreen (SPF 30+)
- **Cooling**: Light scarf for neck sun protection

**Fabric priorities**: Cotton, linen, moisture-wicking synthetics

**Color preferences**: Light colors reflect heat

**Hydration**: Emphasize water bottle

### Extreme Heat: Above 32¬∞C (90¬∞F)
**Characteristics**: Very hot, potential heat danger

**Critical Items:**
- **Clothing**: Minimal, light, loose-fitting
- **Tops**: Tank tops, very light shirts
- **Bottoms**: Shorts, light dresses
- **Sun protection** (critical):
  - Wide-brimmed hat (essential)
  - Sunglasses (essential)
  - High SPF sunscreen (50+)
  - UV-protective clothing if extended sun exposure
- **Cooling accessories**:
  - Cooling towel
  - Portable fan
  - Water bottle (2L+)

**Fabric essentials**: Moisture-wicking, light-colored, breathable

**Activity adjustments**: Limit midday outdoor activities, seek shade

**Safety**: Risk of heat exhaustion, emphasize hydration

## Precipitation Handling

### Light/Occasional Rain (10-30% chance, <5mm/day)
**Strategy**: Minimal rain gear, prepare for possibility

**Recommended Items:**
- Compact travel umbrella
- Water-resistant jacket or windbreaker
- Quick-dry clothing as primary wardrobe

**Optional**: Rain cover for backpack/bag

**Note**: "May not need but good to have"

### Moderate Rain (30-60% chance, 5-15mm/day)
**Strategy**: Expect rain, pack accordingly

**Essential Items:**
- Sturdy umbrella or rain jacket (choose based on activities)
- Water-resistant footwear or waterproof shoe covers
- Waterproof bag or dry bag for electronics
- Quick-dry clothing throughout packing list

**Consider**: Rain pants if significant outdoor activities

### Heavy/Frequent Rain (60%+ chance, >15mm/day)
**Strategy**: Rain is primary concern

**Critical Items:**
- Quality rain jacket (breathable, waterproof)
- Rain pants or waterproof trousers
- Waterproof boots or shoes + backup pair
- Multiple plastic bags or dry bags for electronics/documents
- Waterproof backpack cover
- Quick-dry everything (no cotton)

**Activity planning**: Indoor alternatives needed

**Note**: "Expect to get wet, plan accordingly"

### Monsoon/Tropical Storm Season
**Special considerations:**

**Essential:**
- Full rain suit (jacket + pants)
- Multiple waterproof bags
- 2 pairs of shoes (one always drying)
- Moisture-absorbing packets for luggage
- Waterproof phone case

**Clothing choices:**
- Synthetic only (cotton won't dry)
- Extra sets (items may not dry between uses)

**Important**: Check seasonal patterns before trip

## Humidity Considerations

### Low Humidity (<30%)
**Characteristics**: Dry air, comfortable but dehydrating

**Considerations:**
- Fabrics: Any work well
- Skin: Moisturizer essential
- Hydration: Increase water intake
- Comfort: Generally comfortable temperatures feel better

**Packing additions:**
- Lip balm
- Moisturizer
- Extra water bottle capacity

### Moderate Humidity (30-60%)
**Characteristics**: Comfortable range

**Considerations:**
- Standard packing recommendations apply
- No special accommodations needed
- Fabrics: All types comfortable

### High Humidity (60-80%)
**Characteristics**: Sticky, uncomfortable, affects temperature perception

**Impact on temperature**: 25¬∞C with 70% humidity feels like 30¬∞C

**Fabric priorities:**
- Breathable natural fibers (cotton, linen)
- Moisture-wicking synthetics
- Avoid heavy synthetics that trap moisture

**Clothing adjustments:**
- Looser fits for air circulation
- Fewer layers
- More frequent clothing changes
- Quick-dry essential

**Accessories:**
- Sweat-wicking headband or hat
- Extra handkerchiefs/small towels
- Deodorant
- Anti-chafing products

**Packing strategy**: Pack more tops (may need to change)

### Extreme Humidity (>80%)
**Characteristics**: Oppressively humid, constant perspiration

**Critical adaptations:**
- **Fabric**: Only moisture-wicking or linen
- **Quantity**: 50% more tops than normal (will sweat through)
- **Laundry**: Plan frequent washing
- **Drying**: Items may not fully dry (pack extras)
- **Footwear**: Breathable, water-resistant (feet will sweat)

**Essential additions:**
- Antibacterial/antifungal powder
- Extra socks and underwear
- Breathable shoes + sandals

**Comfort items:**
- Portable fan
- Cooling towels
- Excessive water capacity

## UV Protection Requirements

### Low UV (Index 0-2)
**Typical conditions**: Winter, high latitude, overcast

**Protection**: Minimal needed
- Optional sunglasses
- No special clothing needed

### Moderate UV (Index 3-5)
**Typical conditions**: Spring/fall, temperate climates

**Protection**: Standard precautions
- Sunglasses
- Sunscreen SPF 15-30 for extended outdoor time
- Hat optional

### High UV (Index 6-7)
**Typical conditions**: Summer, lower latitudes

**Protection**: Significant precautions needed
- Sunglasses (essential)
- Sunscreen SPF 30+ (essential)
- Hat for extended outdoor time
- Consider sun-protective clothing

**Timing**: Seek shade 10am-4pm

### Very High UV (Index 8-10)
**Typical conditions**: Tropical, high altitude, summer

**Protection**: Aggressive sun protection essential
- Sunglasses (100% UV) essential
- Sunscreen SPF 50+ essential, reapply every 2 hours
- Wide-brimmed hat or cap essential
- Sun-protective clothing for extended exposure
- Seek shade whenever possible

**Special items:**
- Rash guard for water activities
- UV-blocking shirt for hiking

### Extreme UV (Index 11+)
**Typical conditions**: Tropical peaks, high altitude, equatorial

**Protection**: Maximum protection critical
- All items from Very High level
- Consider full sun-protective clothing
- Mineral-based sunscreen (more effective)
- Face covering (buff/gaiter) for extreme exposure
- Minimize midday outdoor time (10am-4pm)

**Safety**: Risk of sunburn in <15 minutes

## Seasonal Layering Strategies

### Spring (March-May, Sept-Nov Southern Hemisphere)
**Challenge**: Variable temperatures, day-to-night swings

**Strategy**: Versatile layering
- Base: Short and long sleeve options
- Mid: Light sweater or cardigan
- Outer: Medium jacket (removable)
- Accessories: Scarf, light gloves available

**Key**: Easy to add/remove layers throughout day

### Summer (June-August, Dec-Feb Southern Hemisphere)
**Challenge**: Heat management, sun protection

**Strategy**: Minimal layering, sun focus
- Base: Light, breathable clothing
- Outer: Sun protection only (hat, sunscreen)
- Layers: Very light cardigan for AC spaces only

**Key**: Keeping cool while protecting from sun

### Fall (Sept-Nov, March-May Southern Hemisphere)
**Challenge**: Cooling temperatures, rain possibility

**Strategy**: Moderate layering, rain prep
- Base: Long sleeves increasingly
- Mid: Sweaters and light fleece
- Outer: Medium jacket, rain capability
- Accessories: Hat, scarf, gloves for late fall

**Key**: Preparing for cooler weather

### Winter (Dec-Feb, June-August Southern Hemisphere)
**Challenge**: Cold protection, staying warm

**Strategy**: Heavy layering system
- Base: Thermal underwear
- Mid: Fleece or wool
- Outer: Heavy insulated coat
- Accessories: Full cold weather set

**Key**: Multiple layers for warmth, trap air

## Extreme Weather Preparations

### Heat Wave Conditions
**Indicators**: Temperatures >35¬∞C (95¬∞F), heat advisories

**Critical items:**
- Minimal, loose clothing
- Wide-brimmed hat (essential)
- High SPF sunscreen (essential)
- Cooling towel
- Large water bottle (2L+)
- Electrolyte packets

**Activity adjustments**: Indoor midday, outdoor early/late

**Safety**: Heat stroke risk, emphasize hydration and rest

### Cold Snap/Winter Storm
**Indicators**: Temperatures <-10¬∞C (14¬∞F), wind chill, blizzard

**Critical items:**
- Full winter gear (boots, coat, gloves, hat)
- Multiple layers (thermal + mid + outer)
- Hand/foot warmers
- Face covering (balaclava or scarf)
- Emergency items (blanket, whistle)

**Safety**: Frostbite risk, limit exposure, know symptoms

### Tropical Storm/Hurricane
**Indicators**: Hurricane season, storm warnings

**Critical items:**
- Full waterproof gear
- Multiple waterproof bags
- Emergency supplies (flashlight, first aid)
- Copies of documents in waterproof case
- Extra batteries/power bank

**Planning**: Know evacuation routes, monitor weather

**Recommendation**: Consider trip rescheduling

### Wildfire/Poor Air Quality
**Indicators**: Fire season, air quality alerts

**Critical items:**
- N95 masks or respirators
- Goggles or wrap-around glasses
- Indoor activities planned
- Medications (asthma inhalers, etc.)

**Activity adjustments**: Mostly indoor, minimize outdoor exposure

### Monsoon Season
**Indicators**: Seasonal heavy rains, flooding risk

**Critical items:**
- Full rain gear (jacket, pants, boots)
- Multiple waterproof bags
- Quick-dry everything
- Anti-fungal powder
- Multiple shoe pairs

**Planning**: Flexible itinerary, expect delays

## Climate Zone Recommendations

### Tropical (Hot year-round, high humidity)
**Examples**: Southeast Asia, Caribbean, Central America

**Year-round packing:**
- Light, breathable clothing
- Strong sun protection
- Rain jacket (afternoon showers common)
- Moisture-wicking fabrics
- Antibacterial items

**Special**: Modest dress for temples/religious sites

### Arid/Desert (Hot days, cool nights, low humidity)
**Examples**: Middle East, Southwest US, parts of Africa

**Key items:**
- Sun protection (extreme)
- Layering (significant day/night temperature swings)
- Light, loose clothing for day
- Warmer layer for evening
- Extra moisturizer

**Special**: Modest dress in many regions

### Temperate (Four distinct seasons)
**Examples**: Most of Europe, Northern US, Japan

**Season-dependent**: Follow seasonal guidelines above

**Key**: Check specific month and weather forecast

### Mediterranean (Mild, wet winters; hot, dry summers)
**Examples**: Southern Europe, California, parts of Australia

**Summer** (May-Sep): Hot and dry, sun protection
**Winter** (Nov-Mar): Mild and wet, rain gear

### Continental (Extreme seasonal variation)
**Examples**: Central US, Central Asia, Eastern Europe

**Summer**: Very hot (25-35¬∞C), sun protection
**Winter**: Very cold (-20 to -5¬∞C), full winter gear

**Key**: Dramatic seasonal differences, pack accordingly

### Polar/Subpolar (Cold year-round)
**Examples**: Iceland, Alaska, Scandinavia

**Even summer**: Cool (5-15¬∞C), need warm layers
**Winter**: Extreme cold (< -20¬∞C), specialized gear

**Key**: Always pack warm, even in "summer"

## Special Considerations

### High Altitude (>2500m / 8000ft)
**Effects**: 
- Cooler temperatures (drops ~6¬∞C per 1000m)
- Stronger UV radiation
- Lower oxygen (affects exertion)

**Packing adjustments:**
- Warmer clothes than lowland equivalent temp
- Higher SPF sunscreen
- Lip balm with SPF
- Layers for temperature swings

**Examples**: Denver, Cusco, Lhasa, Swiss Alps

### Coastal vs. Inland
**Coastal**: More moderate, humid, breezy
**Inland**: More extreme, drier, less wind

**Packing impact**: Coastal slightly more comfortable, less extreme gear needed

### Urban Heat Island
**Effect**: Cities 2-5¬∞C warmer than surrounding areas

**Packing**: Account for additional heat in city exploration

## Integration with Other Rules

### Cultural Norms Override Climate
**Example**: Hot destination with modest dress requirements
- Climate says: Tank tops and shorts
- Culture says: Shoulders and knees covered
- **Solution**: Light, breathable but modest clothing (linen long sleeve, loose pants)

### Activity Requirements Override Comfort
**Example**: Cold destination with swimming
- Climate says: Winter coat
- Activity says: Swimsuit
- **Solution**: Both included, plan for temperature transitions

### Budget Constraints Affect Options
**Example**: Extreme cold destination, low budget
- Optimal: High-tech layering system ($500)
- Budget: Thrift store layers + hand warmers ($50)
- **Solution**: Recommend budget-friendly layering, prioritize essentials

## Summary

**Core principles:**
1. **Temperature is primary** but not only factor
2. **Humidity affects comfort** significantly
3. **Sun protection varies** by latitude, season, altitude
4. **Layering provides flexibility** for variable conditions
5. **Extreme weather requires special preparation**
6. **Climate must integrate** with culture and activities

**Remember**: 
- Weather forecast is specific and actionable
- Climate data is backup for far-future trips
- Always research, never assume
- Present findings clearly with packing implications
- Consider feel-like temperature (wind chill, humidity)
