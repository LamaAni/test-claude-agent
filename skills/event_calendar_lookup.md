# Event Calendar Lookup Skill

## Purpose
Research seasonal events, festivals, holidays, peak tourist seasons, and special occurrences that might affect travel plans and packing needs.

## When to Use
- All trips (festivals can significantly impact experience)
- To inform user of opportunities or challenges
- When planning wardrobe (special event attire)
- To explain crowding or pricing

## Input Parameters
- **Destination**: City/country/region
- **Travel dates**: Specific dates or month
- **Interests**: Cultural events, sports, religious observances

## Research Areas

### 1. Major Festivals and Events
**Look up:**
- Cultural festivals (Carnival, Oktoberfest, etc.)
- Music festivals
- Film festivals
- Food and wine festivals
- Arts events
- Sporting events (Olympics, World Cup, marathons)

**Impact on travel:**
- Crowds and accommodation availability
- Price increases
- Dress codes or special attire
- Booking requirements
- Street closures and transportation changes

### 2. Public and Religious Holidays
**Research:**
- National holidays (businesses/attractions closed)
- Religious observances (Ramadan, Easter, Diwali, etc.)
- School holidays (affects family travel crowds)
- Bank holidays

**Effects:**
- Shop/restaurant/attraction closures
- Reduced transportation services
- Family-heavy crowds
- Local travel peaks

### 3. Peak Tourist Seasons
**Identify:**
- High season (most crowded, expensive)
- Shoulder season (moderate crowds, good weather)
- Low season (least crowded, cheapest)

**Factors:**
- School vacation periods
- Weather patterns (dry season, ski season)
- Major events
- Regional patterns (winter sun seekers, summer beach)

### 4. Weather-Related Seasons
**Note:**
- Hurricane/typhoon season
- Monsoon season
- Wildfire season
- Winter storm season
- Best weather window

**Impact:** Safety and packing considerations

### 5. Local Special Occurrences
**Check for:**
- Marathon or race (road closures)
- Political events (protests, elections, summits)
- Construction or renovations at major sites
- Temporary closures
- Special exhibitions or shows

## Research Process

### Step 1: Check Tourism Calendars
**Sources:**
- Destination tourism board websites
- "Events in [City] [Month]" searches
- Festival databases
- Travel guides

### Step 2: Verify Event Dates
**Important:** Festivals often change dates year to year
- Confirm exact dates for travel year
- Note if event spans multiple days
- Check if before, during, or after travel dates

### Step 3: Assess Impact
**For each event:**
- **Positive:** Unique experience, cultural immersion, excitement
- **Negative:** Crowds, higher prices, bookings difficult
- **Neutral:** Ongoing but doesn't affect most travelers

### Step 4: Determine Packing Implications
**Consider:**
- Special clothing needed (traditional dress, formal attire)
- Crowd navigation (secure bags, comfortable shoes)
- Weather for outdoor events
- Camera/documentation needs

## Quick Reference: Global Peak Seasons

**Europe:**
- Peak: June-August (summer holidays)
- Shoulder: April-May, September-October
- Low: November-March (cold, except Christmas/New Year)

**Caribbean:**
- Peak: December-April (dry season, winter escape)
- Hurricane: June-November (avoid)
- Low: May, September-November

**Southeast Asia:**
- Peak: November-March (dry, cool season)
- Low: April-October (hot, monsoon)
- Varies by country

**South America:**
- Peak: December-February (summer), varies by region
- Patagonia: November-March (summer there)

**Australia/New Zealand:**
- Peak: December-February (summer)
- Ski season: June-September

## Summary
**Key Takeaways:**
1. **Always check** event calendars for destination
2. **Major festivals** require advance booking (6+ months)
3. **Peak season** = crowds + high prices + great weather usually
4. **Religious observances** significantly affect travel experience
5. **Special events** can be highlights or hindrances
6. **Packing adjustments** often needed for events
7. **Transparent communication** about both pros and cons
8. **Booking urgency** varies drastically by season/events

**Remember:** Events can make or break a trip - research them thoroughly!
